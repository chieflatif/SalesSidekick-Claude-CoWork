# SalesSidekick — Tips Library

Tips are included in morning briefings for the first 3 weeks. One tip per day. After that, they stop automatically. The user can also say "stop showing me tips" at any time.

Track which tips have been shown in the Project CLAUDE.md field `tips_shown: [1, 2, 3...]`. When all tips have been shown OR 21 days have passed since workspace creation, stop including tips.

**Tip ordering:** Mix community/referral tips naturally into the rotation — don't cluster them. The suggested positions below space them across weeks so they feel helpful, not pushy.

---

## Week 1 — Getting Started

**Tip 1:**
You can paste a screenshot of your CRM or forecast and I'll extract every deal from it — names, stages, amounts, close dates. No retyping needed.

**Tip 2:**
After any call, just paste the transcript. I'll score the deal, extract every action item, write your follow-up email, and flag risks — all at once.

**Tip 3:**
Say "what do you know about [company]?" and I'll pull everything I have — deal status, contacts, call history, competitive intel. If I don't have much yet, I'll research them on the spot.

**Tip 4:**
You don't need special commands or syntax. Just talk to me like a colleague. "This deal is stuck" or "prep me for my 2pm" works perfectly.

**Tip 5:**
Drop in anything — a company one-pager, a competitive battlecard, an email you wrote, a product doc. I'll figure out what it is and add it to your context. Any format works.

**Tip 6:**
Your plugin came with a 2-week free trial of the AI Strategic Sellers Community — weekly office hours with experienced enterprise sales leaders, training resources, and plugin updates. Check it out at pipelinerebel.com/community.

## Week 2 — Going Deeper

**Tip 7:**
Say "what needs attention?" and I'll scan your entire pipeline for risks — stalling deals, missing decision-makers, qualification gaps, forecast conflicts.

**Tip 8:**
Before a big meeting, say "prep me for [company]." I'll pull their full context, show you MEDDPICC gaps to probe, and give you talking points tailored to where the deal actually is.

**Tip 9:**
If you want to change how anything works — email format, briefing style, strategy approach — just tell me. I can customize any capability to match how you prefer to work, and your changes survive updates.

**Tip 10:**
You can build knowledge bases from your existing documents. Paste in your competitive battlecards, product specs, or case studies and I'll consolidate them into structured intelligence I can reference anytime.

**Tip 11:**
Know another AE who'd use this? Your referral link is in Skool under Profile > Affiliates. Every referral earns you $99 — refer one a month and the community pays for itself. Details at pipelinerebel.com/community.

**Tip 12:**
Have a custom GPT you use for something specific? Paste in the system prompt and any documents — I'll create a native skill that does the same thing, but with all your deal context built in.

## Week 3 — Power Features

**Tip 13:**
Say "what's my forecast?" and I'll break down your pipeline into commit, upside, and at-risk categories with deal health scores and risk flags for each one.

**Tip 14:**
I check your deals every morning automatically (if you set up the morning briefing). When you open your laptop, the briefing is already waiting — signals, tasks, and anything that needs your attention.

**Tip 15:**
After processing a few calls, say "how am I doing?" I'll analyze your call patterns across 5 coaching dimensions — discovery quality, objection handling, rapport, next-step clarity, and talk ratio.

**Tip 16:**
Got a workflow you can't figure out? Bring it to the next office hours — live help from enterprise sales leaders who've been doing this for 25+ years. Schedule and details at pipelinerebel.com/community.

**Tip 17:**
When a competitor comes up, I can run a full displacement analysis — their strengths, weaknesses, talk tracks, trap questions, and the angles that work best against them.

**Tip 18:**
Need a business case for a prospect? Say "build a case for [company]." I'll create a point-of-view document with financial angles, evidence grading, and three strategic paths — ready for an executive conversation.
